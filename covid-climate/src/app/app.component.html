Achieving the climate goals

We have seen that the Corona pandemic ensured that significantly less CO2 was emitted worldwide. However, the question remains, what good it has done to our climate. In the foreseeable future, the regulations will be loosened, and eventually lifted altogether. But what if - as of today - we would simply continue with the containment measures? Could these measures also be used to help us meet our climate goals? We set up two prognosis lines, that give insight into just that:

  • Continuation with regulations
  • Continuation without regulations

The y-axis depicts the amount of CO2 emissions per day, while the x-axis shows the years. The white graph represents the global CO2 of an average day from 1750 to 2020. From there, our prognosis graphs begin. The data for the prognosis for continuing without regulations, comes from the source [1]. Since originally a higher CO2 emission number for 2020 than the actual one was expected, this forecast line starts at that higher value. Based on this data, we calculated a forecast for continuing without regulations by mapping this years percentage decrease in CO2 emissions to the data from [1].
